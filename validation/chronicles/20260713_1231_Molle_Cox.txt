THE DEATH OF MOLLE COX
7/13/2026 12:31:44 PM

Molle Cox, a humble settler of the fledgling Dowsby outpost, lived his brief days amid the harshness that beset the colony. He bore no appointed trade, yet his record bore the marks of many a needed skill: medicine, melee, mining, animal‑handling, culinary arts, speechcraft and modest talents in art and botany. Though listed as unemployed, he was ever upon the field seeking sustenance, for the colony’s stores lay empty. On the twelfth of the seventh month, the records show him repeatedly deciding to eat and to drink, following the deterministic need‑priorities that drove the starving folk to hunt, forage and seek water wherever it might be found. He heeded the rumors of a fox raid that threatened the livestock and of a wandering trader who offered oddments, yet his own condition left him unable to trade or defend.
