THE DEATH OF GILES BECKER

Giles Becker, of mid‑age and known among the folk as a steadfast guard, served the fledgling settlement with both sword and needle. Though his primary charge lay in the watchtowers, his deft hand in tailoring earned him frequent visits from Linyeve Green, to whom he promised fine wool coverings and sturdy cloth strips before each setting sun. In the waning days of his life he was recorded making simple, determined choices: to seek food when hunger gnawed, to draw water when thirst rose, and to rest in his modest bed to restore strength. His skill in marksman, art, and medicine, though noted, was ever secondary to the pressing needs of the colony. As the winter grew harsher and the stores ran dry, the colony’s advisers urged all weary souls to the healer’s tent, yet Giles’ health waned beneath the relentless famine.
